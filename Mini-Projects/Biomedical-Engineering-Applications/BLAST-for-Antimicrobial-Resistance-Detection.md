# Biomedical Engineering Applications

## Week 1: BLAST for Antimicrobial Resistance Detection
- **BME problem**: Rapid identification of antibiotic‑resistant bacteria in clinical settings.
- **How bioinformatics helps**:
  - A clinical isolate’s genome is sequenced (e.g., from a blood infection).
  - **BLAST** compares bacterial DNA against a database of known resistance genes (e.g., CARD – Comprehensive Antibiotic Resistance Database).
  - Matches reveal specific resistance mechanisms (e.g., *bla_KPC* for carbapenem resistance).
- **BME device connection**:
  - **Point‑of‑care sequencing**: MinION (Oxford Nanopore) combined with real‑time BLAST analysis.
  - **Result**: Within hours, doctor knows which antibiotic will work → personalized treatment.
- **What I learned**: BLAST is not just for evolutionary biology – it’s a clinical decision tool.
