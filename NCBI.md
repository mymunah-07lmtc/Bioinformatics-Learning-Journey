# NCBI (National Center for Biotechnology Information)

## What is NCBI?
NCBI is a worldwide resource for molecular biology information. It provides free access to a vast collection of databases, literature, and software tools. Researchers use NCBI to find DNA and protein sequences, scientific articles, and more.

## Resources Available
- **GenBank** – A comprehensive public database of nucleotide sequences.  
- **PubMed** – A database of biomedical literature, including research papers and reviews.  
- **BLAST** – A tool for comparing biological sequences (see `BLAST.md`).

## Why Researchers Use It
- Centralized, authoritative source of biological data.  
- Constantly updated with new submissions from labs around the world.  
- Free and open access.

## What I Learned
I learned that NCBI is like the “Google” for molecular biology. I explored GenBank and saw how DNA sequences are annotated with information about the organism, gene name, and function. PubMed is an incredible resource to find original research articles. I realized that without NCBI, modern genomics and molecular biology research would be very difficult to conduct.
