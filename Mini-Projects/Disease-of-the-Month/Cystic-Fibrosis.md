# Disease of the Month

## Week 2: Cystic Fibrosis
- **Genetic cause**: Mutations in the *CFTR* gene (Cystic Fibrosis Transmembrane Conductance Regulator). Most common: **F508del** (deletion of phenylalanine at position 508).
- **Protein effect**: CFTR is a chloride channel. F508del causes misfolding and degradation, reducing chloride transport → thick mucus in lungs and pancreas.
- **Bioinformatics tools used**:
  - **UniProt** (P13569): View CFTR domains (NBD1, TMD, R domain). F508 is in NBD1.
  - **NCBI ClinVar**: Search “CFTR” – lists pathogenic variants with clinical significance.
  - **BLAST**: Compare CFTR across species – F508 is conserved, highlighting its importance.
- **BME applications**:
  - **Small molecule correctors** (e.g., Trikafta) – structure‑based drug design using PDB models.
  - **Gene therapy**: Viral and non‑viral vectors to deliver healthy CFTR to airway epithelia.
  - **Organ‑on‑a‑chip**: Lung‑on‑a‑chip models to test drug efficacy.
