# Protein of the Week

## Week 1: Hemoglobin (Human)
- **Function**: Oxygen transport in red blood cells. Binds O₂ in lungs, releases it in tissues.
- **Structure**: Tetramer (2α + 2β globin chains), each with a heme group containing iron.  
  *PDB ID: 1GZX*
- **Key bioinformatics steps**:
  - Found sequence on **UniProt** (P69905 for α-globin, P68871 for β-globin).
  - Viewed 3D structure on **PDB** – noticed cooperative binding sites.
  - Used **BLAST** to find hemoglobins in other vertebrates (e.g., mouse, zebrafish).
- **Why it matters for me**: Mutations cause sickle cell disease and thalassemia. Understanding hemoglobin is essential for biomedical engineering solutions like gene therapy or artificial blood.
