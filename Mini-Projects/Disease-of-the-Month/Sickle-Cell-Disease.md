# Disease of the Month

## Month 1: Sickle Cell Disease
- **Genetic cause**: Single nucleotide substitution in the β‑globin gene (*HBB*): **GAG → GTG** (Glu6Val).  
  *NCBI dbSNP: rs334*
- **Protein effect**: Valine replaces glutamic acid at position 6 of the β‑chain. This creates a hydrophobic patch that causes hemoglobin polymerization under low oxygen.
- **Databases explored**:
  - **NCBI**: GenBank record for *HBB* gene (NG_000007.3). Viewed the mutation in the sequence.
  - **PubMed**: Found a review on “pathophysiology of sickle cell disease” (PMID: 28435042).
  - **PDB**: Compared normal hemoglobin (1GZX) vs. sickle‑like fiber model (no full fiber available, but looked at mutated β‑chain from 2HBS).
- **Biomedical engineering connection**:
  - **Gene editing**: CRISPR correction of the mutation in hematopoietic stem cells.
  - **Drug delivery**: Hydroxyurea (fetal hemoglobin inducer) – designing better formulations.
  - **Diagnostics**: Microfluidic devices to detect sickled cells under hypoxia.
