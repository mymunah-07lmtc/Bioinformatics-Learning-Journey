# Protein of the Week
## Week 2: Green Fluorescent Protein (GFP)
- **Function**: Bioluminescence – emits green light when excited by blue/UV light.
- **Origin**: Jellyfish *Aequorea victoria*.
- **Structure**: β-barrel (11 strands) with a chromophore in the center.  
  *PDB ID: 1GFL*
- **Bioinformatics exploration**:
  - **UniProt** entry P42212 shows post‑translational cyclization that forms the chromophore.
  - **BLAST** against other cnidarians revealed homologous fluorescent proteins (e.g., dsRed from coral).
- **Application in BME**: Used as a reporter gene to track cell movement, gene expression, and protein localization in living organisms. Also a building block for biosensors.
