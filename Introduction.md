# Introduction to Bioinformatics

## What is Bioinformatics?
Bioinformatics is an interdisciplinary field that combines biology, computer science, mathematics, and statistics to analyze and interpret biological data. It involves the development of algorithms, databases, and software tools to understand biological processes at the molecular level.

### Why It Matters
- Modern biology produces massive amounts of data (e.g., DNA sequencing, protein structures).  
- Bioinformatics helps organize, analyze, and visualize this data efficiently.  
- It enables discoveries that would be impossible with traditional lab methods alone.

### Applications
- **Genomics**: Assembling and annotating genomes, identifying mutations.  
- **Drug discovery**: Predicting drug targets and designing new therapeutics.  
- **Disease diagnosis**: Finding genetic markers for diseases like cancer or rare disorders.  
- **Personalized medicine**: Tailoring treatments based on an individual’s genetic profile.
